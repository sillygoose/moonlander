Control the thrust by using the throttle slider at the upper right side of the display.

Because of the physics of the lander rocket engine, you can’t select less than 10% thrust (unless you run out fuel).

Control the lander roll angle with the large and small control arrows at the middle right of the display.  Use the large arrows for large rotations of the lander and the small arrows for fine control of your roll angle.

Your instrument panel can only display four instruments at any time but you can select which four of your twelve instruments are displayed.  You can add any of the instruments at the bottom right of the display by selecting the desired instrument (it will start blinking when selected) and then touching the instrument at the top edge of the screen that you wish to replace.

Your instruments are

HEIGHT

Lander elevation above the terrain (ft)

ALTITUDE

Lander elevation above the mean lunar surface (ft)

DISTANCE

How far you need to go to get a meal at McDonalds (ft)

FUEL LEFT

Your remaining fuel (lbs).  You will receive a low fuel warning when 200 lbs of fuel remain.

WEIGHT

The mass of the lander and the remaining fuel and is measured using Earth gravity as the reference point (lbs)

THRUST

Current thrust generated by your lander descent engine (lbs)

ANGLE

The roll angle of the lander relative to the vertical axis (degrees)

VER VEL

Lander speed in the vertical direction (ft/sec)

HOR VEL

Lander speed in the horizontal direction (ft/sec)

VER ACC

Lander acceleration in the vertical direction (ft/sec^2)

HOR ACC

Lander acceleration in the horizontal direction (ft/sec^2)

SECONDS

Mission time (seconds)

 

Moonlander remembers your successful landings on the lunar surface as well as the crashed landers you have left there.  Be careful not to burn any flags with the flame from your descent engine as they are fragile and will be destroyed.

Take care when landing near McDonalds, you would not want to crash into it and miss out on the bonus mini-game.

Shake the tablet to bring up a dialog to end the game early. The original Moonlander ran continuously until the PDP‑11 was reset or powered down.
